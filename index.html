I am a theoretical physicist and dedicated PhD student in High Energy Physics. With my curiosity and passion for science, I am currently pursuing my doctorate at King's College London, where I am part of the Theoretical Particle Physics and Cosmology Group. Before joining King's for my doctorate, I have graduated with a Bachelor and Master of Science with distinction from RWTH Aachen University. My academic journey has also taken me to UC Berkeley, where I spent part of my Master's.

In research, I am especially interested in understanding what nature looks like at the smallest scales, that is, the fundamental particles and their interactions. In this realm of physics, which is commonly described as High Energy Physics, I am trying to contribute towards some of the outstanding puzzles in Particle Physics. My research focusses on Dark Matter, and the Electroweak Hierarchy problem.

I invite you to explore my work here, and please feel free to reach out with any enquiry you may have.
